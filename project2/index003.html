Courier



Courier is a monospaced slab serif typeface designed to resemble the output

from a strike-on typewriter. The typeface was designed by Howard "Bud" Kettler

in 1955, and it was later redrawn by Adrian Frutiger for the IBM Selectric

Composer series of electric typewriters. Although the design of the original

Courier typeface was commissioned by IBM, the company deliberately chose

not to secure legal exclusivity to the typeface and it soon became a standard

font used throughout the typewriter industry. Because IBM deliberately chose

not to seek any copyright, trademark, or design patent protection, the Courier

typeface cannot be trademarked or copyrighted and is completely royalty free.
